FROSTBOUND
IRENA CLINIC THREAD
Screenwriting Adaptation by Lisa Creveling

Adapted from the in-engine Yarn Spinner implementation to foreground character voice,
branch logic, escalation, and playable decision beats.

============================================================
INT. CLINIC DOOR - DAY
============================================================

IRENA
You're not the Doctor.

PLAYER CHOICES
A. He's away for a few days. I'm covering.
B. What do you need?

IRENA
My hands have been giving me trouble. Hard to grip things.
I need to be seen.

PLAYER CHOICES
A. I need to ask you a few questions first.
   GO TO: INTERVIEW
B. Come down to the clinic.
   GO TO: ADMIT

============================================================
INT. CLINIC DOOR - RETURN VISIT
============================================================

IRENA
Still no bed available?

PLAYER CHOICES
A. Actually, yes. Come in.
   GO TO: ADMIT
B. How are you holding up?
   GO TO: INTERVIEW

============================================================
INT. CLINIC - INTERVIEW
============================================================

IRENA
Ask what you need to ask. I haven't got all day.

PLAYER CHOICES
A. Who are you?
   GO TO: BIO
B. What are your symptoms?
   GO TO: SYMPTOMS
C. I've heard all I've needed to hear.
   GO TO: DECISION

------------------------------------------------------------
BIO
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
Irena. I handle preservation for the village: salting,
smoking, fermenting. Can't afford to have my hands giving
out on me.

PLAYER
Understood.

RETURN TO: INTERVIEW

------------------------------------------------------------
OCCUPATION / WORLD BUILDING BRANCH
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
I stretch what's left. Seaweed from the low tide, dried herbs,
whatever the hunters bring in. The ships haven't been reliable.
Someone has to fill the gap.

PLAYER CHOICES
A. The ships have been unreliable?
B. How long have you been doing this?

IF A:

IRENA
For months now. I don't know what's happening out there,
and nobody's told me anything useful. All I know is the stores
are lower than they should be.

PLAYER
That's worth knowing. Now about your symptoms—

RETURN TO: INTERVIEW or ADMITTED HUB

IF B:

IRENA
Since I was a girl. My family taught me. It's not glamorous work,
but people eat because of it. That counts for something.

RETURN TO: INTERVIEW or ADMITTED HUB

------------------------------------------------------------
SYMPTOMS
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
Started two, maybe three weeks ago. Hands shake. Arms feel heavy.
Dizzy in the mornings. I thought it was the cold.

PLAYER CHOICES
A. I'd like to take a proper look at you.
   GO TO: DECISION
B. I need to ask a few more questions.
   GO TO: INTERVIEW

============================================================
INT. CLINIC - ADMISSION DECISION
============================================================

IRENA
Well? Am I coming in or not?

PLAYER CHOICES
A. Come in. Let's get you looked at.
   GO TO: ADMIT
B. I have a few more questions.
   GO TO: INTERVIEW
C. Sorry, there's no room right now.
   GO TO: TURNED AWAY

------------------------------------------------------------
ADMIT
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
Let's get this done.

[Patient is admitted to the clinic. Story state advances.]

------------------------------------------------------------
TURNED AWAY
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
So that's it? Nothing to be done?

PLAYER
Get some rest. Eat what you can.

IRENA
Rest. Right. I'll rest when the stores are full and the village
isn't three bad weeks from starving.

IRENA
Thanks for nothing.

[Visit ends. Patient has been turned away.]

============================================================
INT. CLINIC - ADMITTED HUB
============================================================

IRENA
Ask what you need to ask. I haven't got all day.

PLAYER CHOICES
A. Let's talk about your symptoms.
   GO TO: SYMPTOM-SPECIFIC NODE
B. I need you to stand inside the Examinator.
C. Tell me more about your work.
   GO TO: OCCUPATION
D. Nothing right now.

IF B:

IRENA
You want me to stand inside that thing?

PLAYER CHOICES
1. It gives me more information to work with.
2. Only if you're comfortable with it.

IF 1:
IRENA
Fine. Make it quick.

IF 2:
IRENA
Ugh. If it helps, then let's do it.

[Scanner sequence triggers.]

IF D:

IRENA
Hope you're not wasting time.

============================================================
INT. CLINIC - MISDIAGNOSIS FALLOUT
============================================================

IRENA
What did you just give me?

PLAYER CHOICES
A. It should help with the symptoms you described.
B. I believe it's the right course of treatment—

IF A:

IRENA
Should. SHOULD.

IRENA
I told you about the shaking hands, heavy arms, dizzy in the mornings...
and you give me THIS?

IRENA
I preserve food for a living. I know what a bad batch looks like.
I know when something is WRONG.

IRENA
You don't know what's wrong with me. You just GUESSED.

IF B:

IRENA
You BELIEVE. You BELIEVE.

IRENA
Half this village eats because of me. Half this village survives winter
because I don't guess. I measure. I check. I test twice before I trust anything.

IRENA
And you just handed me something and told me to take it?!

IRENA
Get out of my way.

PLAYER
Irena, please — let me re-examine—

IRENA
Don't touch me!

IRENA
I came here because my hands were shaking and I needed help.
Not to be used as a test subject by someone who doesn't even know
where to start.

IRENA
I'm leaving. You're a fraud!

[Patient leaves. Misdiagnosis state is recorded.]

============================================================
LATER VISIT - TURNING ARC
============================================================

IRENA
You're back. Good.

PLAYER CHOICES
A. How are you feeling?
B. I need you to stand inside the Examinator.

------------------------------------------------------------
TURNING CHECK
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
A little better.

PLAYER
Okay, that's an improvement.

IRENA
Yeah, with the pain subsiding I've had so much time to think.
The rationing, the storage, everything.

PLAYER
What about them?

IRENA
We've been rationing wrong.
Holding back too much.
People don't need less.
They need MORE. Haha... right? I should give them way more, I think.

PLAYER
Uh, if you think so, that's more your expertise than mine.

IRENA
Yes... ha.. it was so simple... haha.

PLAYER
Well, that's great... but let's get back to your symptoms, okay?

GO TO: TURNING SLIP

------------------------------------------------------------
TURNING SLIP
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
Symptoms?
No.
This isn't sickness.
This is clarity.

PLAYER
What do you mean?

IRENA
I've spent years measuring. Cutting portions. Stretching scraps.
Always thinking about scarcity.
...
But what if there isn't any?
What if we've just been... wasting time?

PLAYER
Irena.

IRENA
...
You're still thinking like before.
That's the problem.
You don't see it yet.

PLAYER
Step back.

IRENA
...
Forget I said anything.

[Her deterioration is now visible in the dialogue rhythm and fixation.]

------------------------------------------------------------
TURNING SCANNER
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
If it helps, fine.
Just don't waste my time.
...
Though I don't think this machine will tell you anything useful.
You're looking in the wrong place.

[Scanner sequence triggers.]

------------------------------------------------------------
WAITING
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
You're back.
I've been thinking about the stores.
If we stopped holding back, we could move everything faster. No waste.
...
You see that, right?

PLAYER CHOICE
A. I need you to stand inside the Examinator.
   GO TO: TURNING SCANNER

============================================================
ESCALATION
============================================================

IRENA
Took you long enough.

PLAYER CHOICES
A. Irena, we need to talk.
B. Are you feeling alright?

GO TO: ESCALATION A

------------------------------------------------------------
ESCALATION A
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
I feel efficient.
Clear.
No wasted motion.
No wasted thought.
No wasted people.

PLAYER
Irena, that's not normal.

GO TO: ESCALATION B

------------------------------------------------------------
ESCALATION B
------------------------------------------------------------

IRENA
Normal?
Normal is why the village starves.
Normal is why we ration and rot.
...
I won't do that anymore.

PLAYER
Irena—

IRENA
Don't.
Don't slow me down.
You either help...
or you get out of the way.
While I'm still asking.

[Escalation state is recorded.]

============================================================
END STATE - GONE
============================================================

IRENA
Still standing there?
Food rots. People rot.
Haha... I've known all this time... You'll rot just like they did.
They've called out to me... they've told me to take you with me...
Yes, that's it. They demand it now. That you come with me.
You'll finally see it. We've been waiting so patiently.
Ha..ha..hahahaha..

END
